The Standard Model of particles (SM) is the most currently utilized theory of interactions of subatomic particles. It describes all 
matter and forces between them (excluding gravity) as interactions between 12 fermions (6 quarks and 6 leptons) with their 
corresponding anti-fermions, 4 spin-1 gauge bosons and 1 scalar boson as shown in Fig. \ref{fig:SM}. Within this framework exist 3 distinct forces: strong nuclear force, mediated by the gluon ($g$) boson and color charge; electromagnetic (EM) force, mediated by the photon ($\gamma$) boson and weak nuclear force, mediated by the $W^{\pm}$ and $Z$ bosons.
